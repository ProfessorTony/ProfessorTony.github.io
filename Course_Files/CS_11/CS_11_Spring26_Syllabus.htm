üíª Course Description: Your Mission, Should You Choose to Accept It...

Welcome to **Fundamentals of Computer Science**! Ever wondered how your favorite apps are made, how video games come to life, or how computers "think"? This course is your **launchpad** into the exciting world of computer science and the basics or "Fundamentals". We'll explore the basic building blocks of how computers work and maybe dig into a little Python coding, for beginners. No prior coding experience? **No problem!** We start from scratch and build your skills step-by-step. Get ready to transform from a tech user into a tech creator! You'll be surprised at what you can learn.

Official- Course Description:

     This course is designed to introduce the student to the hardware, software, terminology, and uses of modern computer systems. Sociological, physiological, and psychological impacts of computers and technological changes are included. Computer programming and the use of word processing, electronic spreadsheets, database, and presentation software are introduced. (UC, CSU).

üéØ Course Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:

  • Define the most commonly used computer terms.
  • Describe the components and the functions of a modern computer system.
  • Describe, compare, and contrast the various input, output, and storage devices commonly used in modern computer systems.
  • Create, print, modify, and remove files using a modern operating system.
  • Utilize the common functions of electronic spreadsheets, word processors, presentation software, and databases/file managers to create beginning-level files.
  • Plan, flowchart, analyze, and create computer programs using a high-level programming language as well as the three patterns of structured programming or object-oriented techniques.
  • Utilize knowledge of the effects of technology on society and individuals physically and psychologically.

üí° Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:

  • **Demonstrate** the ability to create computer programs using a high-level computer language as evaluated by the creation and submission of programs matching the assigned specifications.
  • **Demonstrate** the ability to discuss the sociological, physiological, and psychological impacts of technological change.
  • **Demonstrate** the ability to utilize a word processor, electronic spreadsheet, presentation software, and a database/file manager to create beginning-level files.
  • **Demonstrate** the ability to identify advantages and disadvantages of current hardware devices and software used for input, processing, output, storage, and communication.
  • **Demonstrate** the ability to identify and define 150 to 200 terms commonly used in the Computer Science discipline.
